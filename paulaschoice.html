Paula's Choice, Game Changer For Acne

About three or four months ago, my skin started breaking out like never before. I just could not keep up with it for the life of me. All my previous skincare steps started failing me, and I had no idea what to do. I was going through so many pimple patches a day I was starting to wonder why no one's made a pimple patch face mask. I had been ranting to a coworker about it and she had suggested a few items to me, but especially emphasized an exfoliant from Paula's Choice.

It was cheap and I was desperate so I went for it. My friend had explained to me that you should start out using it only twice a week, and that it may send you through something called a purge phase where basically your skin is gonna look a whole lot worse before it gets better. I was luck enough that I never experienced that mysel, but the results were almost instant. I had used the exoliant just one night after work and woke up the next morning to notice a difference in the appearance of my breakouts and the way they felt. Did they shrink half the size or disappear entirely? No, but I could tell there was a difference and couldn't wait for my next use.

As time went on, my breakouts seemed to finally come to a stop and I was, for the most part, only dealing with scarring aftermath from the long war with my face. I had moved my usage up 3 times a week and kept it at that pace, afraid to over use it, for a while.

Recently my face began going through another phase of breakouts I just couldn't control once again. Desperate to save face (pun intended) I decided to start using Paula's Choice Exfoliant everyday. The bottle does intially instruct to use apply daily, however between botht the advice I had received from my friend and the research I did online seeing other people's experience, it all pointed to not using daily despite it suggesting that, which is why I had never done it for so long. But again, I was desperate. I had stopped using my on the spot treatment as I felt it was no longer working and didn't like the way it dried out my face, and stopped using my moisturizer because whenever I'd use my moisturizer after the exfoliant my face would look way too oily, even though it's my understanding that exfoliants can dry out your face.

After making all these changes, it began to work wonders on my face yet again. All but one stubborn pimple had all disappeared over the next couple of days and I, again, was left only to some scarring. I'm hoping this time my face stays at peace, but even if it doesn't, my Paula's Choice Exfoliant isn't going anywhere.
